Timeshare scams cost Americans millions of dollars annually. Understanding how these scams work is your first line of defense.

Common Timeshare Scam Tactics

1. The Initial Hook

  • Unsolicited calls or emails promising free vacations
  • "Exclusive" offers that expire within 24 hours
  • Claims of heavily discounted luxury properties
  • Promises of guaranteed rental income

2. The Pressure Tactics

  • Extended high-pressure sales presentations
  • "Today only" special pricing
  • Aggressive salespeople who won't take "no" for an answer
  • Emotional manipulation about family memories

3. The Contract Trap

  • Complex legal language hiding important terms
  • Undisclosed maintenance fees and assessments
  • Perpetuity clauses that bind future generations
  • Misleading information about resale value

4. The Exit Scam

  • False promises to help you sell your timeshare
  • Upfront fees for "guaranteed" buyers
  • Fake timeshare exit companies
  • Transfer schemes that leave you still liable

  • Never pay upfront fees for promises of sales or transfers
  • Get everything in writing and review with a lawyer
  • Take time to research the company thoroughly
  • Check for complaints with the Better Business Bureau
  • Don't give in to high-pressure sales tactics

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Unsolicited contact from timeshare companies
  • Requests for upfront fees or wire transfers
  • Promises of guaranteed returns or rental income
  • Pressure to sign immediately without review
  • Offers that seem too good to be true
